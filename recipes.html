Cooking Truffles


Truffle hunters and Master Chefs have joined together to delight you with wonderful cooking recipes.
Loved and desired by all connoisseurs from across the globe, the White Truffle is too often replaced by his lower quality peers, or even worse by artificial products, folow us in this adventure of taste!



Cauliflower crostini with salsa caterina


Ingredients for 4 people :

1 head cauliflower, cut into florets
2 tbsp of salt
3 tbsp of Salsa Caterina
8 slices crusty Italian bread
1 clove garlic, cut in half Bring
a large pot of water to boil.


Cooking instructions :

Add the salt and the cauliflower, and cook until very tender, about 10 minutes. Drain the cauliflower very well, and place it in a large bowl. Add the"Salsa Caterina" and mash well with a fork. Meanwhile, toast the bread, and rub with garlic. Arrange the bread on a platter, and spread the cauliflower mixture into each slice. Buon appetito ! From Tartuflange



Omelette withTruffle


Ingredients for 4 people :

2 teaspoon butter
3 eggs
2 tablespoons grated Parmigiano cheese
Black Summer Truffle (whole)
20g salt and pepper


Cooking instructions :

In a small bowl, gently whisk the eggs. Add the Parmesan cheese, salt and pepper, the truffle in slices and the truffle juice of the jar. In a medium saute pan, heat the butter over medium heat.Pour the egg mix into the hot saute pan. Once the eggs are almost fully cooked, fold the omelette in half and cook 2 minutes longer until eggs are set. Serve immediately. From Tartuflange



Asparagus and Cheese Fondue with Truffle


Ingredients for 4 people :

16 fresh asparagus
1 jar of Cheese Fondue

Black Summer Truffle


Cooking instructions :

Wash the asparagus and cut the white parts of the stalk. Bring a pot full of water to a boil and cook the asparagus for about 10 minutes. Drain the asparagus very cerefully and arrange them on the serving dish while hot. In a little pot, pour the cheese fondue and warm it for 2 to minutes. Pour the hot fondue cheese on the asparagus and serve immediately, while hot. From Tartuflange
